The Problem

Why e-waste is the crisis hiding in your drawer.

Every year, billions of phones, laptops, and appliances reach end-of-life — yet the vast majority end up in landfills or informal dump sites, leaching lead, mercury, and cadmium into the soil and water.

The E-Cycle campaign challenges individuals, schools, businesses, and local governments to see e-waste not as trash but as a treasure trove of recoverable materials and a platform for circular economic opportunity.

Our name captures the dual mission: embrace the electronic cycle of repair, reuse, and responsible recycling — and recognise that every single spark, every act of responsible disposal, adds up to system-level change.

// What counts as E-Waste?
📱
Smartphones & Tablets — contain gold, silver, cobalt, and rare earths
💻
Computers & Laptops — recoverable aluminium, copper, and circuit boards
🔋
Batteries & Chargers — lithium cells require specialised safe handling
📺
TVs & Appliances — often contain hazardous CRT glass or refrigerants
Our Three R's

The pillars of E-Cycle

01
🔄
Reduce & Repair First
Challenge the throwaway culture. Advocate for device longevity, right-to-repair policies, and responsible purchasing. The greenest device is the one you already own.
02
🔁
Reuse & Redistribute
Donate functional devices to schools, community centres, and digital equity programmes. One person's outdated gadget is another's gateway to opportunity.
03
♻️
Responsible Recycling
When devices reach true end-of-life, ensure they go to certified e-waste recyclers — not informal dumpsites. Map, promote, and expand collection points in every community.
